Welcome to the Animal Farm!

Where you can find fun facts about your most common animals!!

Lion

Lions are the only cats that live in groups, called prides, and they often hunt together as a team.

Elephant

Elephants are the largest land animals and have remarkable memories, capable of remembering water sources and migration routes for decades.

Tiger

Tigers are the largest species of wildcat and are known for their distinctive orange fur with black stripes, which help them camouflage in tall grass.

Giraffe

Giraffes have the longest necks of any land animal, but surprisingly, they have the same number of neck vertebrae as humans—just seven!

Zebra

Every zebra’s stripes are unique, just like human fingerprints. No two zebras have identical stripe patterns.

Panda

Pandas are primarily bamboo eaters, consuming up to 40 pounds of bamboo a day, but they’re technically members of the Carnivora order.

Kangaroo

Kangaroos can leap over 3 meters in a single bound and use their large, powerful tails for balance while hopping.

Grizzley Bear

Grizzly bears can run up to 35 miles per hour, but they often prefer to spend their time fishing in rivers or foraging for food

Leapord

Leopards are incredibly strong and agile, capable of carrying prey up into the trees to avoid scavengers.
